12 marks

“Which had more impact?”

Used in: America, 1920–1973

What the Examiner Wants

The question gives you two bullet points (e.g. two events, policies, or developments) and asks which had MORE impact. You must write about BOTH bullets, not just one. The examiner wants to see you explain the impact of each and then COMPARE them to reach a judgement.

Level Descriptors (Simplified)

Level Marks What You Need
Level 4 10–12 Detailed analysis of both bullets with specific evidence, plus a clear and supported comparison explaining which had more impact
Level 3 7–9 Explains both bullets with relevant knowledge but the comparison is underdeveloped or missing
Level 2 4–6 Describes both bullets but without clear explanation of impact or comparison
Level 1 1–3 General statements or only addresses one bullet
— 0 Nothing relevant

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Read both bullet points — Underline the key terms in each. Note the specific topic area they relate to.
  2. 2
    Plan: evidence for each bullet — For each bullet, jot down 2–3 specific facts (dates, statistics, names, events) that show impact.
  3. 3
    Write paragraph 1: Bullet A — Explain the impact of the first bullet with specific evidence. Make it clear HOW and WHY it affected people’s lives.
  4. 4
    Write paragraph 2: Bullet B — Explain the impact of the second bullet with specific evidence. Begin to compare: was this impact bigger, smaller, or different in nature?
  5. 5
    Write paragraph 3: Judgement — Directly answer which had MORE impact. Explain your reasoning by comparing the scale, duration, or significance of each.

Timing

Plan
2 min Bullet A
5 min Bullet B
5 min Judgement
4 min

The judgement paragraph is crucial for Level 4. Don’t skip it even if you’re running short on time — even a brief comparison can push you up a level.

Paragraph Templates

Bullet A

“[First development/event] had a significant impact on [group of people] because…”

“For example, [specific evidence with dates/statistics]…”

“This meant that [explain the effect on people’s lives]…”

Bullet B

“However, [second development/event] also had an important impact…”

“For instance, [specific evidence]…”

“This affected people’s lives because [explain]…”

Judgement

“Overall, I believe [bullet A/B] had more impact because…”

“While [other bullet] was important, [chosen bullet] had a [wider/longer-lasting/more significant] effect because…”

Annotated Model Answer

Which of the following had more impact on the lives of black Americans? • The Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955–56) • The Civil Rights Act (1964). Explain your answer with reference to both bullet points. [12 marks]

The Montgomery Bus Boycott had a significant impact on the lives of black Americans because it was the first successful large-scale protest against segregation in the South. For 381 days, 40,000 black residents of Montgomery refused to use the city buses, costing the bus company 65% of its revenue. The Supreme Court ruled bus segregation unconstitutional in Browder v. Gayle (1956). The boycott proved that non-violent direct action could defeat Jim Crow laws, and it made Martin Luther King Jr. a national leader. However, its direct impact was limited to bus segregation in one city.

The Civil Rights Act of 1964 had a much broader impact because it outlawed racial discrimination across the entire United States. The Act banned segregation in all public places, prohibited employment discrimination based on race, and strengthened voting rights enforcement. It was backed by federal law, meaning businesses and state governments could be taken to court if they discriminated. Within a year, segregation signs came down across the South and black Americans gained legal access to restaurants, hotels, and workplaces that had previously excluded them.

Overall, the Civil Rights Act had more impact because it affected every black American in every state, not just bus passengers in Montgomery. The boycott was a crucial catalyst — without it, the civil rights movement might not have gained momentum — but its direct impact was narrow. The Civil Rights Act turned the principles of the movement into enforceable law, creating permanent change rather than a single local victory. The boycott inspired the movement, but the Act delivered its results.

Examiner: 12/12 (Level 4)

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Only writing about one bullet — Both bullets must be covered. Writing brilliantly about one but ignoring the other caps you at Level 1.
  • ❌
    No comparison or judgement — “Both had an impact” is not a judgement. You must say which had MORE impact and explain WHY, comparing them directly.
  • ❌
    Describing events without explaining impact — The question asks about IMPACT on lives, not what happened. Always connect facts to how people’s lives changed.
  • ❌
    Unbalanced paragraphs — Give roughly equal space to both bullets. A full page on one and two sentences on the other suggests you only revised half the topic.
