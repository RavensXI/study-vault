8 marks

“In what ways were lives affected?”

Used in: America, 1920–1973

What the Examiner Wants

The examiner wants you to explain HOW people’s lives changed as a result of a specific event, development, or policy. Focus on the effects on real people — not just what happened, but what it meant for their daily lives, opportunities, rights, or living standards.

Level Descriptors (Simplified)

Level Marks What You Need
Level 4 7–8 Detailed explanation of multiple ways lives were affected, with specific evidence and clear links to the lives of people
Level 3 5–6 Explains ways lives were affected with relevant knowledge but some points lack development
Level 2 3–4 Describes changes but doesn’t clearly explain how these affected people’s lives
Level 1 1–2 General statements with limited relevant knowledge
— 0 Nothing relevant

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Identify the event/development and the people — Who is the question asking about? Which group of people? What time period?
  2. 2
    Plan 2–3 ways lives were affected — Think about different aspects: economic (money, jobs), social (daily life, freedoms), political (rights, laws), cultural (attitudes, opportunities).
  3. 3
    Write paragraph 1 — Explain the first way lives were affected. Use specific evidence (statistics, examples, named events). Make the link to people’s lives explicit.
  4. 4
    Write paragraph 2 — Explain a different way lives were affected. Again, evidence and clear link to lives. Try to cover a different aspect (e.g. if Para 1 was economic, make Para 2 social or political).

Timing

Plan
2 min Para 1
5 min Para 2
5 min

Focus on depth over breadth. Two well-evidenced ways are better than four vague ones. Always link facts back to how ordinary people experienced the change.

Paragraph Templates

Paragraph 1 (first way lives were affected)

“One way [event/development] affected the lives of [people] was…”

“For example, [specific evidence with dates/statistics]…”

“This meant that [explain impact on daily life]…”

Paragraph 2 (second way lives were affected)

“[Event/development] also changed people’s lives by…”

“For instance, [specific evidence]…”

“As a result, [explain how this affected their opportunities/rights/living standards]…”

Annotated Model Answer

In what ways were the lives of Americans affected by the economic boom of the 1920s? [8 marks]

The economic boom of the 1920s transformed the daily lives of many Americans by making consumer goods affordable for the first time. Mass production techniques, pioneered by Henry Ford’s assembly line, cut the price of a Model T car from $850 in 1908 to $295 by 1927. By 1929, there were 23 million cars registered in America — roughly one for every five people. Hire purchase (buying on credit) meant even working-class families could afford radios, refrigerators, and washing machines. These products saved time on housework and connected people to entertainment and news, changing how they spent their leisure time.

The boom also affected social attitudes and opportunities, particularly for women. The prosperity of the 1920s gave young women the financial independence to challenge traditional expectations. ‘Flappers’ wore shorter skirts, smoked in public, and went out without chaperones — behaviour that would have been scandalous before the war. The number of women in employment rose by 25% during the 1920s, with many working as secretaries, telephonists, and shop assistants in the growing consumer economy. However, these changes mainly affected white, middle-class women in cities — rural women and African Americans saw far fewer benefits from the boom.

Examiner: 8/8 (Level 4)

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Describing the event instead of its effects on lives — “Ford used assembly lines to make cars” is description. You need: “Cheaper cars meant families could travel further for work and leisure, transforming daily life.”
  • ❌
    Being too vague about “lives” — “People’s lives got better” scores poorly. Be specific: which people? How exactly did their lives change? Use statistics and examples.
  • ❌
    Only covering one way — Two developed points is the target for Level 4. One point, even if detailed, limits your mark.
  • ❌
    Ignoring limitations — The best answers acknowledge that not everyone was affected equally. A brief mention of who missed out shows sophisticated understanding.
