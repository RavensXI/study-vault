4 marks

“Describe two…”

Used in: America, 1920–1973

What the Examiner Wants

This is the simplest question on the paper. The examiner wants two separate, relevant things described with enough detail to show you know the topic. Each point gets up to 2 marks: 1 mark for identifying something relevant, and a second mark for adding supporting detail. No analysis or explanation is needed — just clear, factual recall.

Level Descriptors (Simplified)

Marks What You Need
4 Two relevant points, each with supporting detail
3 Two relevant points, but only one has supporting detail
2 Two simple points without detail, or one well-developed point
1 One simple relevant point
0 Nothing relevant

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Identify two things to describe — Read the question. What specific topic is it asking about? Think of two DIFFERENT aspects, features, or examples.
  2. 2
    Write point 1 — State one relevant thing. Then add a second sentence with specific detail (a date, name, statistic, or example) to develop it.
  3. 3
    Write point 2 — State a DIFFERENT relevant thing. Again, add supporting detail. Make sure it’s genuinely a new point, not a repeat of point 1.

Timing

Think
1 min Point 1
2 min Point 2
2 min

This is the quickest question on the paper. Two short paragraphs (2–3 sentences each) is all you need. Don’t waste time writing more — save it for the higher-mark questions.

Point 1

“One [feature/example/problem] was…”

“For example, [add specific detail: date, name, statistic]…”

Point 2

“Another [feature/example/problem] was…”

“This involved [add specific detail]…”

Describe two problems faced by immigrants to America in the 1920s. [4 marks]

Point 1 + Detail

One problem was discrimination in employment. Many immigrants, particularly those from Southern and Eastern Europe, were seen as a threat to American workers’ jobs. They were often forced to accept the lowest-paid and most dangerous work, such as in meatpacking factories or coal mines, because employers could exploit their desperation.

Point 2 + Detail

Another problem was the restriction of immigration through quota laws. The 1921 Emergency Quota Act limited immigration to 3% of each nationality already in the US (based on the 1910 census), and the 1924 National Origins Act reduced this further to 2% based on the 1890 census. This deliberately targeted immigrants from Southern and Eastern Europe, making it much harder for them to enter the country.

Examiner: 4/4

Compare these two responses to the same question. The annotations highlight what separates a 1-mark answer from a 2-mark one.

Describe two features of the Ku Klux Klan in the 1920s. [4 marks]

1-Mark Answer

Vague point, no supporting detail

The KKK was a racist group that was against black people and immigrants.

2-Mark Answer

Clear point + specific detail

One feature of the KKK in the 1920s was its rapid growth in membership. By 1925, the Klan had around 4.5 million members, including politicians, police officers, and judges, which gave it significant political influence across Southern and Midwestern states.

Remember: you need TWO separate points like the strong example for full marks. This shows just one — you’d write a second paragraph on a different feature.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Only making one point — You need TWO separate points for full marks. Even if your first point is brilliant, one point can only score 2 out of 4.
  • ❌
    Stating a point without detail — “Immigrants faced discrimination” gets 1 mark. Adding “They were given the worst jobs in meatpacking factories” gets the second mark. Always add a specific detail.
  • ❌
    Writing too much — This is a 4-mark question. Two short paragraphs (2–3 sentences each) is perfect. Writing half a page wastes time you need for the 8 and 12-mark questions.
