16 + 4 SPaG marks

“How far do you agree?” / Factor Essay

Used in: Conflict & Tension • Health & the People

What the Examiner Wants

The examiner wants you to make a judgement about a statement. You must argue BOTH sides — the factor in the statement AND at least one other factor — then reach a clear conclusion about which matters most.

Level Descriptors (Simplified)

Level Marks What You Need
Level 4 13–16 Complex explanation of stated factor AND other factors, with a clear judgement weighing them against each other
Level 3 9–12 Explains both the stated factor and other factors, but the judgement is missing or weak
Level 2 5–8 Describes relevant information about the topic but doesn’t clearly explain HOW or WHY factors matter
Level 1 1–4 General statements or simple facts with little focus on the question
— 0 Nothing relevant

SPaG Descriptors

Performance Marks What You Need
High 4 Spelling, punctuation and grammar consistently accurate. Wide vocabulary. Paragraphs well structured.
Intermediate 2–3 Generally accurate SPaG with some errors. Reasonable vocabulary. Some paragraph structure.
Low 1 Basic SPaG with frequent errors. Limited vocabulary. Little paragraph structure.

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Read the statement carefully — Underline the factor in the statement (e.g. “the Treaty of Versailles”, “science and technology”). This is the factor you MUST write about first.
  2. 2
    Plan two+ factors — Jot down the stated factor plus 1–2 other factors. For each, note 2–3 specific facts (dates, names, statistics).
  3. 3
    Write paragraph 1: the stated factor — Explain how the factor in the statement is important. Use specific evidence. Link back to the question.
  4. 4
    Write paragraph 2: another factor — Explain a different factor with its own evidence. Compare it to the first factor — is it more or less important?
  5. 5
    Write paragraph 3 (optional): a third factor — Adds complexity but only if you have time. Brief but specific.
  6. 6
    Conclusion: make your judgement — Directly answer “how far” you agree. State which factor you think matters most and WHY it outweighs the others. Don’t sit on the fence.

Timing

Plan
5 min Para 1
7 min Para 2
7 min Conclusion
4 min SPaG
2 min

This is the longest question on the paper. Don’t rush the plan — 5 minutes of planning saves time while writing because you know exactly what each paragraph will say.

Opening (stated factor)

“One reason [statement factor] is important is…”

“For example, [specific evidence]…”

“This shows/suggests that [link to question]…”

Counter-argument (other factor)

“However, [other factor] was also significant because…”

“For instance, [specific evidence]…”

“This challenges the statement because [explain why this factor might matter more/less]…”

Conclusion

“Overall, I [agree/disagree] with the statement to a [large/limited] extent because…”

“Although [other factor] was important, [stated factor] was ultimately [more/less] significant because…”

‘The main reason for the failure of the League of Nations was the absence of the USA.’ How far do you agree with this statement? [16 marks + 4 SPaG]

Stated Factor + Evidence

The absence of the USA was a major reason the League of Nations failed. Without America, the League lost its most powerful potential member, which weakened both its economic sanctions and its moral authority. For example, when Japan invaded Manchuria in 1931, the League could not impose effective trade sanctions because the USA — Japan’s biggest trading partner — was not a member. This meant the League’s main weapon was useless, making it easy for aggressive nations to ignore its rulings.

Other Factor + Comparison

However, the structure of the League itself was also a critical weakness. Decisions in the Assembly and Council required unanimous agreement, which meant any single member could block action. Britain and France, the League’s leading members, were often reluctant to act because they were recovering from the First World War and prioritised their own interests. During the Abyssinia Crisis in 1935, Britain and France secretly agreed the Hoare–Laval Pact to give Mussolini two-thirds of Abyssinia rather than confront him. This suggests the League would have struggled even with American membership, because its own decision-making process was fatally slow.

Judgement

Overall, I agree with the statement to a large extent. While the League’s structure was flawed, these weaknesses were magnified by America’s absence. With the USA inside the League, economic sanctions would have had real teeth and aggressive nations would have faced a united front. The structural problems could have been overcome with strong leadership, but without America there was no leader powerful enough to make the League work.

Examiner: 15/16 + 4 SPaG = 19/20 (Level 4)

Compare these two responses to the same question. The annotations highlight what separates a Level 2 answer from a Level 4.

‘Science and technology has been the main factor in the development of medicine since medieval times.’ How far do you agree? [16 marks + 4 SPaG]

Level 2 Answer

Lists facts without explaining

Science helped medicine get better. Pasteur discovered germs and this was really important for medicine. Before that, people believed in the Four Humours and miasma. Koch found the bacteria that caused diseases like tuberculosis and cholera. Jenner also made a smallpox vaccine earlier, in 1796. These are all examples of how science improved medicine over time.

Level 4 Answer

Explains with specific evidence

Science and technology was a significant factor in the development of medicine. Pasteur’s Germ Theory (1861) proved that microorganisms caused disease, overturning centuries of belief in miasma. This was crucial because it gave doctors a scientific target for the first time — Robert Koch built on this by identifying the specific bacteria causing tuberculosis (1882) and cholera (1883), allowing public health officials to tackle the real causes of epidemics rather than simply cleaning up bad smells.

A full Level 4 answer would continue with a counter-argument (e.g. government action was equally important), then a conclusion with a clear judgement — see the model answer above.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Only writing about the stated factor — You MUST discuss at least one other factor to reach Level 3+. The question asks “how far” you agree, which requires weighing alternatives.
  • ❌
    No judgement in the conclusion — “Both factors were important” is not a judgement. You must say which factor matters MORE and explain why.
  • ❌
    Telling the story without explaining — Describing what happened (narrative) only reaches Level 2. You need to explain WHY factors were important and HOW they connect to the question.
  • ❌
    Ignoring SPaG — 4 marks for spelling, punctuation and grammar is almost a full grade boundary. Use paragraphs, spell key terms correctly, and leave 2 minutes to proofread.
  • ❌
    Spending too long on this question — 25 minutes maximum. Students often overwrite the essay and run out of time for earlier questions worth easy marks.
