8 marks

“Explain two ways… similar”

Used in: Health & the People

What the Examiner Wants

The examiner gives you two things from different time periods (e.g. medieval and Renaissance medicine) and asks you to find SIMILARITIES between them. You need to identify two clear points of comparison and explain each with evidence from BOTH periods. This tests your ability to see patterns across history.

Level Descriptors (Simplified)

Level Marks What You Need
Level 4 7–8 Two developed similarities, each supported by specific evidence from both periods, with clear explanation of HOW they are similar
Level 3 5–6 Two similarities identified with relevant knowledge, but one or both lack full development or evidence from both periods
Level 2 3–4 One similarity explained, or two stated without development
Level 1 1–2 General statements about the topic with limited relevant knowledge
— 0 Nothing relevant

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Identify the two things being compared — What two periods, events, or developments does the question name? Write them both down.
  2. 2
    Find two similarities — Think about: methods, causes, beliefs, results, barriers, or attitudes. What patterns repeat across both periods? Each similarity becomes one paragraph.
  3. 3
    Write paragraph 1: first similarity — State the similarity clearly. Give specific evidence from BOTH periods. Explain why this counts as a genuine similarity, not just a coincidence.
  4. 4
    Write paragraph 2: second similarity — A different similarity, again with evidence from both periods. Make sure it’s genuinely a different point, not the same idea rephrased.

Timing

Plan
2 min Para 1

5 min

The planning stage is critical here. Spend 2 minutes thinking of two distinct similarities before you write. Each paragraph MUST reference both periods mentioned in the question.

Paragraph 1 (first similarity)

“One similarity between [period A] and [period B] is that both…”

“In [period A], [specific evidence]…”

“Similarly, in [period B], [specific evidence]…”

“This shows that [explain the pattern/reason for the similarity]…”

Paragraph 2 (second similarity)

“Another similarity is that [both periods shared]…”

“For example, in [period A], [evidence]…”

“Likewise, in [period B], [evidence]…”

“This suggests that [explain the underlying reason]…”

Explain two ways in which ideas about the causes of disease in the medieval period were similar to ideas in the Renaissance period. [8 marks]

Similarity 1 + Both Periods

One similarity is that people in both the medieval and Renaissance periods believed that God sent disease as punishment for sin. In the medieval period, the Church taught that the Black Death (1348) was a punishment from God, and flagellants whipped themselves in public to show repentance. Similarly, during the Great Plague of 1665, which fell in the Renaissance period, many people still believed the epidemic was divine punishment, and days of fasting and prayer were ordered by the authorities. This shows that religious explanations for disease persisted across both periods, even as new scientific ideas began to emerge.

Similarity 2 + Both Periods

Another similarity is that both periods relied on the theory of the Four Humours developed by Hippocrates and Galen. Medieval doctors diagnosed illness by checking whether a patient’s humours (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, black bile) were out of balance, using techniques such as urine charts and bloodletting. In the Renaissance, despite Vesalius correcting many of Galen’s anatomical errors in 1543, the underlying humoral theory remained dominant. Doctors still prescribed treatments like purging and bleeding to rebalance the humours well into the 1600s. This demonstrates that traditional theories about disease causation were extremely slow to change, even when new knowledge challenged them.

Examiner: 8/8 (Level 4)

Compare these two responses to the same question. The annotations highlight what separates a Level 2 answer from a Level 4.

Explain two ways in which hospitals in the medieval period were similar to hospitals in the early modern period. [8 marks]

Level 2 Answer

Vague similarity, no evidence from either period

Both medieval and early modern hospitals looked after sick people. In medieval times hospitals were run by monks and nuns. In the early modern period hospitals still existed. They were similar because they both treated ill patients and tried to help people get better.

Level 4 Answer

Specific evidence from both periods

One similarity is that hospitals in both the medieval and early modern periods were primarily places of care rather than cure. Medieval hospitals like St Bartholomew’s (founded 1123) focused on providing food, warmth, and prayer for the sick rather than medical treatment. Similarly, early modern hospitals continued this tradition — even after the dissolution of the monasteries in the 1530s, refounded hospitals like St Thomas’s still prioritised nursing care over active medical intervention, because without understanding of germ theory, doctors had few effective treatments to offer.

A full Level 4 answer would add a second distinct similarity with evidence from both periods — see the model answer above.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Writing about differences instead of similarities — Read the question carefully. If it asks for similarities, every point must be about what the two things have IN COMMON, not how they differ.
  • ❌
    Only giving evidence from one period — Each paragraph must reference BOTH periods. Evidence from only one side is incomplete and caps your mark.
  • ❌
    Stating similarities without explanation — “Both used herbal remedies” is a statement. You need to explain it: what herbal remedies, why, and what this tells us about the similarity.
  • ❌
    Making the two similarities too similar — “Both believed in God” and “Both thought disease was divine punishment” are really the same point. Choose genuinely distinct similarities (e.g. one about beliefs, one about treatments).
