8 marks (QWC)

“Discuss…”

Used in: R180 Written Examination

What the Examiner Wants

This is the highest-mark question on the paper and carries a Quality of Written Communication (QWC) assessment. The examiner wants you to consider multiple perspectives or factors, develop each one in detail, and reach a supported conclusion. Your spelling, punctuation, grammar and use of specialist terminology all contribute to the mark.

Marking

Level Marks What You Need
4 7–8 Comprehensive, well-structured discussion with detailed knowledge, multiple perspectives considered, accurate terminology, and a clear conclusion. QWC: fluent writing with few errors.
3 5–6 Good discussion with developed points and some balance, but may lack depth in places or have a weak conclusion. QWC: generally clear with occasional errors.
2 3–4 Some relevant points made but limited development, one-sided argument, or weak use of terminology. QWC: meaning is clear but writing has noticeable errors.
1 1–2 Basic, superficial response with little or no development. QWC: poor spelling, punctuation and grammar that hinder communication.
— 0 No relevant response

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Introduction — Write one or two sentences that show you understand the question and signal that you will consider different sides. For example: “Risk assessments play an important role in sport, but their effectiveness depends on how thoroughly they are carried out.”
  2. 2
    Points in favour / for — Give 2–3 developed points supporting one side of the argument. Each should follow the pattern: point → because → this means → example.
  3. 3
    Points against / alternative view — Give 1–2 developed points presenting the other side or limitations. This shows balance and lifts your answer to the higher levels.
  4. 4
    Conclusion — Weigh up the arguments and give a clear, justified judgement. “Overall, I believe… because…” Never sit on the fence — commit to a position and support it.

Timing

Plan 2 min Write — 8 min Check 2 min

About twelve minutes total. Spend two minutes planning your for/against points, eight minutes writing, and two minutes checking your spelling, punctuation and grammar — QWC marks are easy to lose through careless errors.

Discussion structure

Intro: “[Topic] is an important consideration in sport because [brief context]. There are arguments both for and against [the statement].”

For: “One reason [topic] is important is [point]. This is because [development]. For example, [specific example].”

Against: “However, [counter-argument]. This suggests that [development]. For instance, [specific example].”

Conclusion: “Overall, I believe [judgement] because [justification based on the strongest arguments].”

Discuss the importance of a risk assessment in reducing the risk of sports injuries. [8 marks]

Level 4 (7–8 marks)

Risk assessments are a key part of health and safety in sport, but their effectiveness depends on how well they are carried out and followed up.

One reason risk assessments are important is that they identify hazards before an activity begins. By inspecting the playing surface, equipment and weather conditions in advance, a coach can spot potential dangers such as a waterlogged pitch or damaged goalposts. This means the hazard can be removed or managed before anyone gets hurt, directly reducing the chance of acute injuries such as fractures or concussions.

A second reason is that risk assessments encourage appropriate safety measures to be put in place. For example, if the assessment identifies that a gymnastics session involves a high risk of falls, crash mats and spotters can be provided. Without the assessment, these precautions might be overlooked, increasing the likelihood of injury.

Furthermore, risk assessments help coaches match activities to the ability of participants. By considering factors such as age, fitness level and experience, a risk assessment ensures that beginners are not exposed to advanced drills that could lead to overuse injuries or accidents. This is particularly important in school sport where ability levels vary widely.

However, risk assessments have limitations. They cannot predict every possible hazard — an unexpected collision between two players or a sudden change in weather may still cause injury regardless of planning. Additionally, a risk assessment is only useful if it is acted upon. If a coach identifies a risk but fails to implement control measures, the assessment itself offers no protection.

Overall, I believe risk assessments are very important in reducing sports injuries because they provide a structured way to identify and manage hazards before they cause harm. While they cannot eliminate all risk, they significantly reduce the likelihood of preventable injuries, especially when combined with proper follow-up actions.

Examiner: 8/8 — Comprehensive, well-structured discussion. Three distinct “for” points, each fully developed with specific examples. Balanced with a clear “against” paragraph acknowledging limitations. Strong conclusion with a justified judgement. Accurate use of specialist terminology throughout. QWC: fluent, well-punctuated, no errors.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    One-sided argument — Only writing “for” points without considering the other side caps you at Level 2. You must show balance to reach Levels 3 and 4.
  • ❌
    No conclusion — Ending abruptly without a judgement loses marks. The examiner wants to see you weigh up the arguments and commit to a position.
  • ❌
    Ignoring QWC — Spelling, punctuation and grammar are assessed in this question. Sloppy writing, text-speak or missing full stops can drop you a level even if your content is good.
  • ❌
    Shallow breadth over depth — Listing six brief points is less effective than three well-developed ones. The examiner rewards depth of explanation, not just the number of points.
