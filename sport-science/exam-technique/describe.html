2–3 marks

“Describe…”

Used in: R180 Written Examination

What the Examiner Wants

A describe question asks you to give detail about something — a process, an injury, a factor or a method. You do not need to explain why it happens. Just say what it is and develop the point with specific detail.

Marking

Marks What You Need
2–3 Clear points with development and accurate use of sport science terminology
1 A basic point made but lacking detail or development
0 Incorrect, irrelevant, or no answer given

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Identify the topic — What exactly are you being asked to describe? An injury type, a training method, a component of fitness? Underline the key word in the question.
  2. 2
    Make your point — State the thing clearly using correct terminology. For example: “A sprain is damage to a ligament.”
  3. 3
    Develop with detail — Add specific information to earn the second/third mark. Where does it happen? What does it involve? What does it look like? Use the pattern: Point + Development.

Timing

Describe — 3 min

About three minutes. Write your point, develop it with specific detail, and move on. Don’t slip into explaining why — that is a different command word.

Point + Development

“[Thing] is [what it is]. This involves/includes [specific detail about how, where, or what].”

Describing a process

“First, [step 1 happens]. Then [step 2 happens]. This results in [outcome].”

Describe one type of acute injury. [2 marks]

Full marks

A sprain is damage to a ligament caused by overstretching or tearing (1). This often occurs at the ankle joint when a performer lands awkwardly or changes direction quickly (1).

Examiner: 2/2 — Clear identification of the injury type with accurate terminology, plus specific development about where and how it occurs.

Describe one type of acute injury. [2 marks]

1 mark — no development

A sprain is when you hurt your ankle.

Examiner: The answer names a sprain but doesn’t describe what it actually is (ligament damage) or give any specific detail. “Hurt your ankle” is too vague for the second mark.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Explaining instead of describing — “Describe” means say what it is, not why it happens. Save your reasoning for “explain” questions.
  • ❌
    Lacking specific terminology — Saying “you hurt your leg” instead of “damage to a ligament” loses marks. Use the correct sport science terms.
  • ❌
    Making a point without developing it — One bare statement only earns 1 mark. Always add a second sentence with specific detail to pick up the remaining marks.
