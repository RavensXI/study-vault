3–4 marks

“Explain…”

Used in: R180 Written Examination

What the Examiner Wants

Explain questions require you to give reasons why something happens. You must build cause-and-effect chains — not just state facts, but show how one thing leads to another. Each link in the chain earns a mark.

Marking

Marks What You Need
3–4 Clear cause-and-effect chains with accurate terminology showing full understanding
1–2 Basic point(s) made but lacking development or reasoning
0 Incorrect, irrelevant, or no answer given

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Make your point — State the factor, reason or cause clearly. For example: “A warm up increases blood flow to the muscles.”
  2. 2
    Say “because” or “this means” — Extend the point by explaining the consequence. “This raises muscle temperature and makes them more elastic.”
  3. 3
    Complete the chain — Link back to the question. “This means the muscles can stretch further without tearing, reducing the risk of strains.”
  4. 4
    Repeat if needed — For 4-mark questions, you may need a second developed point or an additional link in the chain.

Timing

Explain — 5 min

About five minutes. Spend the time building clear chains of reasoning. Each “because” or “this means” is another potential mark.

Cause → effect chain

“[Factor] causes [effect] because [reason]. This means [consequence], which leads to [link back to question].”

Two-point explain

“One reason is [point 1] because [development]. Another reason is [point 2], which means [development].”

Explain why a warm up can reduce the risk of injury. [4 marks]

Full marks

A warm up increases blood flow to the muscles (1), which raises muscle temperature and makes them more elastic (1). This means the muscles can stretch further without tearing (1), reducing the risk of strains during the activity (1).

Examiner: 4/4 — Excellent chain of reasoning. Each sentence builds logically on the previous one, moving from cause (increased blood flow) through mechanism (elasticity) to outcome (reduced injury risk).

Explain why a warm up can reduce the risk of injury. [4 marks]

1 mark — no chain of reasoning

A warm up gets your body ready for exercise so you don’t get injured.

Examiner: This is essentially just restating the question. There is no explanation of how or why a warm up reduces injury risk. You need to explain the mechanism — what happens in the body.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Describing instead of explaining — Saying what happens is not enough. You must say why or how it happens. Use connectives like “because,” “this means,” and “which leads to.”
  • ❌
    Breaking the chain — Each point must connect logically to the next. Jumping from a cause to an unrelated outcome without showing the link loses marks.
  • ❌
    Restating the question — “A warm up reduces injury because it stops you getting injured” is circular. You need to explain the mechanism behind the effect.
