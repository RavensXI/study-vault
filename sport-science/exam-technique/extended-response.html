6 marks

Extended Response

Used in: R180 Written Examination

What the Examiner Wants

Extended response questions require a longer, well-structured answer that demonstrates detailed knowledge. You need to cover multiple points, develop each one fully, and use specific examples. The examiner is looking for depth, not just breadth — a few well-developed points beat a long list of shallow ones.

Marking

Level Marks What You Need
3 5–6 Detailed and thorough response with well-developed points, accurate terminology, and clear logical structure
2 3–4 Sound response with some development but may lack detail or consistent use of terminology
1 1–2 Basic response with limited knowledge shown and little or no development of points
— 0 No relevant response

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Introduction — Write one sentence that shows you understand the question. For example: “A warm up has several different components, each serving a specific purpose in preparing the body for exercise.”
  2. 2
    Developed point 1 — Make your first point, then develop it with specific detail. Say what it is, how it works, and why it matters. Use a practical example.
  3. 3
    Developed point 2 — Move to a second point and develop it the same way. Make sure it is clearly different from point 1.
  4. 4
    Developed point 3 — Add a third point if needed. Three well-developed points is usually enough for full marks.
  5. 5
    Link back — End with a sentence that ties your answer back to the question, showing how all your points connect.

Timing

Plan 1 min Write — 6 min Check 1 min

About eight minutes total. Spend one minute planning your points, six minutes writing, and one minute re-reading to check your chains of reasoning are complete.

Extended response structure

“[Topic] involves several key [aspects/components/factors].”

“Firstly, [point 1]. This involves [detail]. For example, [specific example].”

“Secondly, [point 2]. This is important because [development]. In practice, [example].”

“Finally, [point 3]. This means [development and link back to question].”

Explain the different components of a warm up. [6 marks]

Level 3 (5–6 marks)

A warm up has several components that work together to prepare the body for physical activity.

The first component is a pulse raiser, which is a low-intensity cardiovascular activity such as jogging or cycling. This gradually increases heart rate and blood flow to the working muscles, delivering more oxygen and raising body temperature so the muscles become warmer and more pliable.

The second component is mobility exercises, which involve moving joints through their full range of motion. This could include arm circles or hip rotations. These exercises increase the production of synovial fluid in the joints, which lubricates them and reduces friction, lowering the risk of joint injuries.

The third component is stretching, specifically dynamic stretching where muscles are stretched through movement rather than held in a static position. For example, walking lunges stretch the hip flexors and quadriceps. This increases muscle elasticity and prepares the muscles for the specific movements they will perform.

Finally, a warm up should include sport-specific movements that replicate the actions used in the main activity. A footballer might practise dribbling or passing. This activates the neuromuscular pathways used during the activity, improving reaction time and coordination from the start.

Examiner: 6/6 — Four distinct components clearly identified and thoroughly developed. Each point includes what the component is, how it is done, and why it matters. Accurate terminology throughout (synovial fluid, dynamic stretching, neuromuscular pathways). Logical structure from start to finish.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Listing without developing — Writing “pulse raiser, stretching, sport-specific” as a list earns 1–2 marks at most. Each point must be developed with detail and reasoning.
  • ❌
    No structure or paragraphing — A solid wall of text is hard for the examiner to follow. Use paragraphs to separate each point clearly.
  • ❌
    Repeating the same point — Saying “it warms up the muscles” three different ways only counts once. Each point must be genuinely different.
