Religious rivalry, English support for Dutch rebels, Drake’s privateering, and the Treaty of Nonsuch — the causes of war with Spain.

 Lesson 9

Why poverty increased under Elizabeth, attitudes towards the ‘deserving’ and ‘idle’ poor, and the development of the Elizabethan Poor Laws.

 Lesson 11

Elizabethan Exploration

Drake’s circumnavigation, Hawkins and the slave trade, Raleigh’s lost colony at Roanoke, and the new technology that made it all possible.

Elizabethan social hierarchy, the growing wealth of the gentry, grand country houses, and the golden age of Shakespeare and the theatre.

 Lesson 13

The Essex Rebellion

The Earl of Essex’s rise and fall, his disastrous campaign in Ireland, rivalry with Robert Cecil, and his failed rebellion of 1601.

 Lesson 14

Threats to Elizabeth’s Rule

A review of every major threat — Catholic plots, Mary Queen of Scots, Spain, Puritans, and the succession crisis — and how serious each one was.

 Lesson 15

Was It Really a ‘Golden Age’?

Weighing the evidence for and against — cultural brilliance and exploration versus poverty, persecution, and ongoing conflict.
