6 marks

“Discuss…”

Used in: Theme 1 & Theme 2

What the Examiner Wants

This is the first levels-based question on the paper. The examiner wants you to analyse both sides of an issue and reach a conclusion. You must show you can see advantages AND disadvantages, or arguments FOR and AGAINST. One-sided answers cannot reach Level 3.

Level Descriptors (Simplified)

Level Marks What You Need
Level 3 5–6 Detailed discussion of both sides with developed chains of reasoning. A clear, reasoned conclusion that weighs up the arguments.
Level 2 3–4 Some discussion of one or both sides with limited development. May lack a conclusion or give a superficial one.
Level 1 1–2 Basic or vague statements about the topic. Points are listed, not developed.
— 0 Nothing relevant

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Plan (1 minute) — Jot down one argument FOR and one AGAINST. If the question gives a case study, note how your points connect to the business.
  2. 2
    Paragraph 1: One side — Make a developed point with a chain of reasoning (point → explain → impact on business). Use the case study if provided.
  3. 3
    Paragraph 2: The other side — Start with “However…” or “On the other hand…” and make an equally developed counter-argument.
  4. 4
    Conclusion (1–2 sentences) — Weigh up both sides. Which argument is stronger, and why? Don’t sit on the fence — commit to a judgement.

Timing

Plan
1 min Side 1
3 min Side 2
3 min Conclude
2 min

Around 9 minutes total. Spend roughly equal time on each side. The conclusion can be short but must be there — it’s the difference between Level 2 and Level 3.

Side 1 (for)

“One advantage of [X] is [point].”

“This means that [explain why this matters]…”

“As a result, the business would [impact]…”

Side 2 (against)

“However, a disadvantage of [X] is [counter-point].”

“This could lead to [negative consequence]…”

“Therefore, [business name] may find that [impact]…”

Conclusion

“Overall, I think [X] is / is not [the better option] because [strongest reason]. Although [acknowledge the other side], the benefit of [chosen side] outweighs this because…”

Discuss the impact of e-commerce on a small high-street clothing shop. [6 marks]

Side 1 — Positive impact

E-commerce could benefit the clothing shop by allowing it to sell to customers beyond the local area. Setting up an online store means the business is no longer limited to customers who walk past the shop. This could significantly increase revenue because the potential market is now national or even international, rather than just the local high street.

Side 2 — Negative impact

However, e-commerce also creates new challenges for a small shop. Large online retailers like ASOS and Amazon can offer lower prices because they buy in bulk and have automated warehouses. A small shop may struggle to compete on price, and the cost of building a website, managing delivery logistics, and handling online returns could be significant. This could reduce profit margins rather than increase them.

Conclusion

Overall, e-commerce is likely to have a mixed impact. While it offers the opportunity to reach more customers, the small shop would need to find a way to stand out from larger competitors — perhaps by offering unique or locally-made products that aren’t available from big retailers. Without a clear niche, the costs of going online could outweigh the benefits.

Examiner: 6/6 (Level 3) — both sides developed with reasoning, conclusion makes a judgement linked to context

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    One-sided answers — Writing only about advantages (or only disadvantages) caps you at Level 2 at best. The word “discuss” means the examiner expects BOTH sides.
  • ❌
    No conclusion — Many students forget the conclusion. Even two sentences weighing up the arguments can push you from 4 marks to 6. Always finish with “Overall…”
  • ❌
    Listing points without development — “It’s cheap. It’s quick. It reaches more people.” is three undeveloped points, not one developed chain. Pick ONE advantage and explain it fully.
  • ❌
    Sitting on the fence — “It could be good or bad, it depends” is not a conclusion. Commit to a position and explain why one side is more convincing than the other.
