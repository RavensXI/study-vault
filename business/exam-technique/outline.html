2 marks

“Outline one…”

Used in: Theme 1 & Theme 2

What the Examiner Wants

The examiner wants you to identify a relevant point and then develop it. Think of it as “state + expand.” Your first sentence names the point; your second sentence explains it briefly or gives an example.

Marking

Marks What You Need
2 Identification of a relevant point (1) + linked development or example (1)
1 Identification of a relevant point with no development
0 Nothing relevant

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Identify your point — State the point clearly in one sentence. What is the reason, advantage, disadvantage, or factor?
  2. 2
    Develop it — Explain what you mean or give a brief example. Use “This means that…” or “For example…” to connect your second sentence.

Timing

Identify
1 min Develop
1 min

Two minutes at most. Two sentences is ideal — don’t write a paragraph.

Standard outline

“One [reason/advantage/way] is [point]. This means that…”

With example

“One [reason/advantage/way] is [point]. For example,…”

Outline one reason why a business might use market research. [2 marks]

Identify + Develop

A business might use market research to find out what customers want (1). This means the business can develop products that meet customer needs, reducing the risk of low sales (1).

Examiner: 2/2 — clear identification with linked development

Outline one advantage of operating as a sole trader. [2 marks]

1 mark — no development

You get to keep all the profits.

Full marks

The owner keeps all the profits (1). This is an incentive to work hard because the more successful the business is, the more money the sole trader earns (1).

Examiner: The first answer identifies a valid point but never develops it. Always add a “this means…” sentence.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Listing two separate points — The question says “outline ONE.” If you list two points without developing either, you can only get 1 mark. Always develop your single point.
  • ❌
    No development — Stating “market research helps a business” without explaining how or why only earns 1 mark. The second mark needs a linked explanation.
  • ❌
    Writing too much — Two sentences is enough for 2 marks. Don’t spend 5 minutes on a question worth less than a “Discuss.”
