3 marks

“Explain one way…”

Used in: Theme 1 & Theme 2

What the Examiner Wants

The examiner wants a chain of reasoning. You need to identify a point, explain it, and then develop or apply it further. Think of it as building a logical chain: Point → Explanation → Impact.

Marking

Marks What You Need
3 Identification (1) + explanation (1) + further development or application to context (1)
2 Identification + explanation, but no further development
1 Identification of a relevant point only
0 Nothing relevant

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Identify the point — State clearly what the “one way” is.
  2. 2
    Explain why or how — Use connectives like “This is because…” or “This means that…” to explain the point.
  3. 3
    Develop the impact — Extend the chain. What is the effect on the business? Use “As a result…” or “Therefore…” If the question gives a case study, apply your answer to it.

Timing

Identify
1 min Explain
1 min Develop
1 min

Three minutes maximum. Three sentences is the ideal length — one per mark.

The 3-sentence chain

“One way is [point].”

“This means that [explanation of why/how]…”

“As a result, the business would [impact on business]…”

With case study context

“One way [business name] could [do X] is by [point].”

“This would [explanation]…”

“Therefore, [business name] would benefit because [impact]…”

Explain one way in which a break-even chart could help a new business. [3 marks]

Point → Explain → Impact

A break-even chart shows the business how many units it needs to sell to cover its costs (1). This is useful because the entrepreneur can see whether the sales target is realistic before investing money (1). As a result, the business can avoid launching a product that would make a loss, which reduces the financial risk for the owner (1).

Examiner: 3/3 — clear chain of reasoning with each sentence building on the last

Weak vs Strong: Spot the Difference

Explain one way in which technology could help a small business compete with larger rivals. [3 marks]

1 mark — no chain

Technology can help a business sell things online and reach more customers.

Full marks

A small business could use social media marketing to reach a wider audience (1). Unlike traditional advertising, social media is free or low-cost, which means the business does not need a large marketing budget to compete (1). This allows the small business to build brand awareness and attract customers who might otherwise only buy from well-known larger competitors, increasing its revenue (1).

Examiner: The weak answer jumps from point to impact without explaining the link. The strong answer builds: point → why → what happens.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Breaking the chain — Each sentence must logically follow the previous one. “E-commerce is good. It saves time. Customers are happier.” has no connective tissue. Use “This means…”, “As a result…”, “Therefore…”
  • ❌
    Explaining two ways instead of one — The question says “one way.” Listing two points without developing either scores a maximum of 1 mark. Depth beats breadth.
  • ❌
    Ignoring the case study — If the question is about a specific business, refer to it by name. Generic answers cap you at 2 marks when context is provided.
  • ❌
    Repeating the same point in different words — “Social media helps marketing. This helps them market better.” That is the same idea twice. The third sentence must push further — what is the actual impact on profit, sales, or competitiveness?
