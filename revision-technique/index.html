Revision Techniques

Evidence-based strategies that actually work. Each technique is backed by cognitive science research and tailored to GCSE History revision.

Foundation Techniques

Start early — use these throughout your revision
Active recall

Retrieval Practice

Test yourself, don’t just re-read. Brain dumps and self-quizzing beat highlighting every time.

 Scheduling

Spaced Repetition

Spread it out over days and weeks. Short sessions with gaps between them lock knowledge in long-term memory.

 Visual learning

Dual Coding

Combine words and visuals to remember more. Timelines, flowcharts, and comparison tables stick better than text alone.

 Summarising

Knowledge Organisers

Condense each topic onto one page. The act of creating it — from memory — is where the learning happens.

Exam Preparation

Final weeks before the exam — sharpen and apply
Deep thinking

Elaborative Interrogation

Ask “why?” and “how?” to deepen understanding. Build causal chains that turn facts into explanations.

 Mixed practice

Interleaving

Mix topics and question types per session. It feels harder, but the research shows it produces stronger recall.

 Exam readiness

Timed Exam Practice

Write like it’s the real thing. Practise under timed conditions and self-mark against the level descriptors.
