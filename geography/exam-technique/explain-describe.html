4 marks

“Explain two…” / “Describe…”

Used in: Paper 1 & Paper 2

What the Examiner Wants

Two types of 4-mark question appear. “Explain two…” wants two developed points, each with a chain of reasoning (point + development = 2 marks each). “Describe…” wants a detailed description of a process, pattern, or distribution, with 4 separate valid points.

Marking

Type Marks What You Need
Explain two 2 + 2 Two points, each with a developed explanation (1 for point + 1 for development, twice)
Describe 4 × 1 Four separate valid points or two developed descriptions

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Identify the command word — “Explain” means give reasons (why/how). “Describe” means say what you see or what happens (the what, not the why).
  2. 2
    For “Explain two” — Write two short paragraphs. In each: state the point, then develop it with a “because…” or “which means…” chain. Don’t write three explanations — the examiner will only mark the first two.
  3. 3
    For “Describe” — Use specific detail: data, place names, compass directions, or process sequences. General statements like “it’s everywhere” will not score.

Timing

Explain / Describe — 5 min

About five minutes. Just over a minute per mark. Don’t write more than you need — two developed points or four clear descriptive statements.

“Explain two”

Point 1: One [reason/factor] is [point] (1). This means that [developed explanation / consequence] (1).

Point 2: Another [reason/factor] is [point] (1). This leads to [developed explanation / consequence] (1).

“Describe the distribution”

“[Feature] is mainly found in [location/direction] (1). There is a concentration in [specific area] (1). Very few are found in [area] (1). An exception is [named example] (1).”

Explain two reasons why people continue to live near tectonic hazards. [4 marks]

Full marks

Point 1: The soil near volcanoes is extremely fertile because volcanic ash breaks down into nutrient-rich minerals (1). This allows farmers to grow crops successfully, providing food and income for local communities (1).

Point 2: Many people cannot afford to move away from hazard-prone areas (1). In LICs like Haiti, poverty means families have no financial resources to relocate, so they must stay and accept the risk (1).

Examiner: 4/4 — two clearly separate reasons, each developed with a chain of reasoning.

Describe the distribution of tropical storms. [4 marks]

Full marks

Tropical storms are found between approximately 5° and 30° north and south of the equator (1). They form over warm ocean water in the Atlantic, Pacific and Indian Oceans (1). They do not form on the equator itself because the Coriolis effect is too weak (1). The highest concentration is in the western Pacific, where they are called typhoons (1).

Examiner: 4/4 — specific latitudes, named oceans, exception at the equator, and a named concentration area.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Describing when asked to explain — “Describe” = say what happens. “Explain” = say why/how it happens. Mixing these up costs marks.
  • ❌
    Writing three points for “Explain two” — The examiner only marks the first two. A third point wastes time and cannot earn marks.
  • ❌
    Vague descriptions — “They are found everywhere” scores nothing. Use specific locations, compass directions, latitudes, and named places.
