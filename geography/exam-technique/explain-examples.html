6 marks

“Explain…” (with examples)

Used in: Paper 1 & Paper 2

What the Examiner Wants

This is a levels-based question. The examiner wants a detailed explanation that uses specific case study knowledge and examples to support your points. You must show chains of reasoning — not just list facts, but explain how and why things happen. The best answers use named places, statistics, and precise details from case studies.

Level Descriptors

Level Marks What You Need
Level 3 5–6 Detailed explanation with thorough understanding. Chains of reasoning with specific, accurate geographical knowledge. Named examples, data, and case study detail.
Level 2 3–4 Clear explanation with some understanding. Some development but may lack specific detail or case study knowledge.
Level 1 1–2 Basic statements with limited explanation. Points listed without development.

Step-by-Step Formula

  1. 1
    Plan (1 minute) — Jot down 2–3 points you want to make. For each, note a specific example or piece of case study detail.
  2. 2
    Write 2–3 developed paragraphs — Each paragraph should: make a point, explain it with a chain of reasoning, and support it with a specific named example or statistic.
  3. 3
    Use PEE or PEE+ — Point → Explain → Example (with specific case study detail). Add a further development if you can.

Timing

Plan Write — 7 min

About 8 minutes total. Spend 1 minute planning, then 7 minutes writing 2–3 well-developed paragraphs.

PEE+ structure (repeat 2–3 times)

Point: One [reason/effect/factor] is [statement].

Explain: This is because [chain of reasoning].

Example: For example, in [named case study/place], [specific detail/statistic].

Extend: This shows that / This means / As a result, [further development].

Explain why deforestation of tropical rainforests has global consequences. [6 marks]

Level 3 (6/6)

Deforestation contributes to climate change because trees absorb CO₂ during photosynthesis. When they are cut down and burned, this stored carbon is released back into the atmosphere as a greenhouse gas. In the Amazon, deforestation releases an estimated 0.5 billion tonnes of CO₂ per year, accelerating global warming.

Rainforests also play a crucial role in the water cycle through transpiration. Trees release moisture into the atmosphere, which forms clouds and generates rainfall. When large areas are deforested, rainfall decreases — not just locally but in regions that depend on moisture carried by wind patterns. This can affect agriculture thousands of miles away.

Biodiversity is also lost on a global scale. Tropical rainforests contain over 50% of the world’s species, many of which have not yet been discovered. Deforestation destroys habitats, pushing species toward extinction. This matters globally because potential medicines, foods and genetic resources are lost before they are even studied.

Examiner: 6/6 — Three well-developed points with specific detail (0.5bn tonnes CO₂, 50% of species). Clear chains of reasoning throughout.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌
    Listing without explaining — “Deforestation causes climate change, soil erosion and flooding” is a list. You need to explain how each one happens.
  • ❌
    No case study detail — Generic answers without named places or statistics cannot reach Level 3. Always include specific examples.
  • ❌
    Writing too little — Two sentences will not score 6 marks. You need 2–3 developed paragraphs to demonstrate thorough understanding.
